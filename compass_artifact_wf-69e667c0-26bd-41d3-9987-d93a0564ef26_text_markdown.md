# The 20 NYC AI startups you should feature first

The YC classes of 2023–2026 produced an extraordinary cluster of AI companies operating in or near New York City, but a clear top tier separates itself on funding, founder story, and sheer "wow factor." **Browser Use ($17M raised, 78K GitHub stars) and CellType (Yale spinout simulating human biology, cited by Sundar Pichai) sit at the top**, followed closely by Hera (100K-person waitlist in eight weeks) and Mastra ($13M seed from 120+ investors including Paul Graham). Below is the definitive ranking, with a full intelligence card for each company.

The startups span every category your show could want: consumer products kids can use (Pixley AI), AI that literally fights scammers (BeeSafe AI), robots with personality (Lightberry), a team trying to "kill Chrome" (Meteor), and two brothers aged 20 and 24 automating Wall Street audits (Denki). The list is ordered by a composite score weighting unique use case, funding strength, general-audience appeal, visual demo potential, and compelling founder narrative.

## #1 · Browser Use — The weekend experiment that raised $17 million

**What they do:** Open-source framework that lets AI agents navigate and control websites like a human. Started as a weekend prototype; now provides browser-as-a-service infrastructure with anti-detect capabilities, CAPTCHA solving, and proxies across 195 countries. Powers the viral AI agent Manus (by Butterfly Effect).

**Why they're interesting:** The archetypal "overnight open-source rocket ship." Two founders built a prototype in four days. It hit **78,000+ GitHub stars**, making it one of the fastest-growing open-source projects ever. They are the invisible infrastructure behind many headline-grabbing AI agents.

**Funding & investors:** **$17M Seed** led by **Felicis Ventures**, with A Capital, Nexus Ventures, Y Combinator, **Paul Graham** (personal investment), Liquid2, SV Angel, and Pioneer Fund.

**Traction signals:** 78K+ GitHub stars. 15,000+ active developers. 5,000-person Discord. Fortune 500 companies in production. Launched the same week as OpenAI's Operator.

**Video series appeal: 9/10.** The "4-day prototype to $17M" arc is a startup fairy tale. Demo potential is high—show an AI agent browsing the web, booking flights, filling out forms. The Manus connection lets you tie them to the biggest AI agent story of 2025. Best opener: "What if a weekend side project became the plumbing under every AI agent on the internet?"

**Best outreach angle:** Pitch the "picks and shovels" narrative—they're powering Manus and hundreds of other agents behind the scenes. Founders Magnus Müller and Gregor Zunic can demo live agent browsing. Reach via their active Discord or YC contacts.

**Why they're interesting:** This is the single most "sounds impossible" company on the list. **Traction signals:** Sundar Pichai public mention. Top 10 pharma partnerships. ICML publication. Validated cancer hypothesis. BiopharmaTrend feature story.

Best opener: "What if we could cure cancer without ever testing a drug on a real person?"

**Best outreach angle:** Reach David van Dijk through Yale CS or his YC profile. The DeepMind collaboration and Sundar Pichai citation are irresistible hooks. Offer to film at their lab or with their pharma partners.

## How to sequence your first season

The strongest opening episode is **Lightberry or Pixley AI**—both deliver instant visual spectacle (talking robots, kids' drawings becoming cartoons) and emotionally resonant founder stories. **CellType** and **BeeSafe AI** work best as "mind-blown" episodes where the audience learns something that sounds impossible. **Browser Use**, **Mastra**, and **Sim** anchor a "builder" arc about the infrastructure powering every AI agent. **Flick**, **Hera**, **Koyal**, and **Absurd** naturally cluster into a creative AI mini-series. And **Slashy**, **Pixley AI**, and **Meteor** form a compelling "youngest founders" trilogy.

Three themes cut across nearly every company worth highlighting. First, **open source as growth engine**: Browser Use, Mastra, Sim, Manufact, and Tinfoil all used GitHub stars as their primary customer acquisition channel, a strategy that would have been unthinkable five years ago. Second, **the "second act" pattern**: Mastra's Gatsby founders, KERNEL's $500M-exit CTO, and Flick's Instagram Stories engineer are all betting their next decade on AI—each brings hard-won lessons from previous wins. Third, **extreme youth**: an 18-year-old Forbes lister (Slashy), 19-year-old MIT dropouts (Pixley AI), 20-year-old CTO brothers (Denki), and multiple college dropouts collectively represent the youngest and most technically ambitious generation of YC founders yet.

The startups most likely to have raised significant additional funding by the time you begin filming are CellType (W26, likely closing seed imminently), Hera (revenue doubling monthly), and Lightberry (a16z Scout involvement often precedes larger rounds). Prioritize outreach to these three alongside Browser Use and Mastra, whose founders already have public-speaking experience and will be easiest to book.
